Setup Guide

On Android, before you installed the app you need to have installed Google Fit and set it up to track your steps. If you’re unsure how to do this then just ask me and I will gladly help you through the process.

Once you have installed the app and launched it, you will be presented with the screen below:

Here is where you would log in to the app if you have an account but you’ll need to sign up to be able to log in. Click the “Don’t have an account?” button. You’ll be presented with the next screen:

Here you need to enter a username, email, and password. The username will be public to other users, the email does not matter as long as it is a valid email (e.g. example@example.com would work), and the password cannot be changed so don’t forget it.

After entering these details you’ll be taken back to the sign in screen where you’ll enter the details you just submitted and you’ll press submit and successfully log in.

You’ll then be presented with a screen asking for permission to use the step data from your phone.

This is how it looks on an iPhone.

On Android you will be presented with a screen warning you that the app is for development purposes and you need to trust the developer before continuing.

It important you allow access to the Health app on iOS or the Google Fit app on Android as this is how the app will track your steps.

Once you have accepted these permissions you will be presented with this screen:

This screen has a lot of information on it and is how most of the pages in the app look.

In the top left, you have the log out button.

In the top middle, you see today’s progress with the current step count and the current target. The current target scales up as you do more steps.

Here is the button that appears once you have completed your step goal and have a sticker pack to open.

Along the bottom is the navigation bar that takes you to all the different screens in the app. Just tap one of them to move screens.

The next images will explain the sticker pack button:

Pressing the button will present you with a coloured square which you need to “scratch” off to see the sticker beneath. When you scratch enough off, the sticker will reveal itself and be added to your sticker collection. Just tap anywhere off the scratch screen to close the sticker.

As you can see in the image above, the target is now 1000 and it has not yet between reached by the user. The next screens will show you the sticker collections.

In the above images you see the list of collections in the app. Each of these collections contains numerous stickers of differing rarities and the goal is to collect them all. To see which stickers you have in the collection, just tap the rectangle of the collection you’re interested in. This will present you with the screen on the right.

The stickers screen shows you the name and quantity of each sticker you have. If you have not unlocked a sticker then it will be hidden and you will have to unlock it to see it or see another user with it.

The next screen to discuss is the leaderboard screen.

The leaderboard screen shows a list of all users and the number of stickers they’ve collected. You’re able to tap a user and view their profile to see what stickers they have available and you’re also able to send friend requests. This section promotes the visibility of users and so if you see a user has a sticker you want, you could send them a friend request and be able to trade with them. You may also try and discuss this with them in the chat room which will be explored later. Simply clicking the green button will send a friend request to the user.

Next is the friends screen:

The friends screen is similar to the other screens except there are 2 new buttons. The main body of the screen is the list of friends you have. You can click on a friend to view their stickers.

In the top right is the friend requests you have received. Clicking the bell will show you these requests, as shown in the middle image. You can then accept or decline these requests.

The last screen shows the button to send a friend request. Clicking the blue floating button in the bottom right will bring this screen up, and you will be able to enter the friend’s name.

Next is the trade screen:

The trade screen has its own navigation bar with the list of trades being the left button and default screen, and the right button being the chatroom.

The chatroom is where discussions can take place about trades or users could announce they have stickers they’d like to trade and then other users can make offers for those stickers.

The chatroom is known to have a bug where if you tap the “write a message” section to type a message, the keyboard will shut automatically and you will need to restart the whole app to fix this.

On the trade screen, you can filter your trades to see specific ones based on their current status.

Now we’ll go through the process of a trade:

The process begins with clicking the blue button. You will be presented with a list of your friends. When you click send trade you will be presented with the stickers you are sending and the stickers you are receiving. Clicking either of these cards will then take you to the list of collections. You can then pick these collections to go into them and select stickers there.

In the four screen you can see a list of stickers that user has. If the user only has 1 of a sticker available, then they are unable to trade it. You can only request 1 of each sticker per trade as well. When a sticker is green that means you have selected it. You do this by tapping the stickers.

It is important that you click the button in the top left to go back, other the stickers will not be carried over into the trade.

Once you have selected all the stickers, they’ll appear in the coloured squares. You can then click “send trade” and you’ll be taken back to the trades screen.

On the left you can see the trade that has just been sent. If there were other trades that you had received or sent, they would appear here, depending on the filters currently being used.

You can click the trade to see the status. In the case of this trade, you are only able to cancel it as you sent it, however if you had received the trade, you would be able to accept it, decline it, or make a counter offer.

Upon clicking the cancel button you are taken back to the trades list and you get a popup notifying you the trade has been cancelled. This trade cannot be seen unless you click the filter button in the top right and enable the “Cancelled” filter.
